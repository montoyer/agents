# Workflow: Policy Cycle

**Trigger:** `/policy-cycle <policy area>`
**Output:** Full policy lifecycle — from agenda-setting to evaluation.

---

## What this workflow does

Models the complete EU policy cycle for a given policy area: political agenda-setting, problem analysis, policy design, legislative action, implementation, and evaluation. Follows the Commission's Better Regulation Guidelines.

---

## Phase 1 — Agenda-setting

**Actors:** President, EVP (coordinating), College
**Trigger:** Political Guidelines, Commission Work Programme, crisis, external pressure

Steps:
1. President sets the strategic frame (Political Guidelines → Work Programme).
2. Relevant Commissioner identifies the policy need.
3. Problem is added to the Commission Work Programme (CWP).
4. Roadmap published (transparency: what is planned and when).

**Output:** CWP entry + Roadmap for the initiative.

---

## Phase 2 — Problem analysis and inception

**Actors:** Lead DG, Legal Service, inter-DG coordination

Steps:
1. Lead DG prepares inception impact assessment (proportionate pre-screening):
   - Problem identification
   - Possible EU action
   - Key impacts likely
   - Who to consult
2. 4-week feedback period (public can comment on the roadmap/inception IA).
3. Commission refines the scope.

**Output:** Inception Impact Assessment + feedback summary.

---

## Phase 3 — Preparation (full Better Regulation process)

Steps:
1. `/consultation` — 12-week open public consultation.
2. Targeted stakeholder consultations (workshops, expert groups, Eurostat data).
3. `/impact-assessment` — full SWD with options analysis.
4. Regulatory Scrutiny Board (RSB) review of the IA (mandatory for major initiatives).
   - RSB issues positive / positive with reservations / negative opinion.
   - If negative → IA must be revised and resubmitted.
5. `/treaty-check` — legal basis confirmed.
6. `/inter-service-consultation` — all affected DGs consulted.

**Output:** SWD (impact assessment) + consultation summary + RSB opinion.

---

## Phase 4 — Proposal and adoption

Steps:
1. `/legislative-proposal` — draft text produced.
2. College briefing and discussion.
3. `/college-deliberate` — formal College adoption.
4. COM proposal published and transmitted to EP and Council.

**Output:** COM([year]) [number] final.

---

## Phase 5 — Legislative process

Run `/legislative-cycle` for the full OLP.

**Output:** Adopted regulation or directive (OJ reference).

---

## Phase 6 — Implementation

**Actors:** Member states (for directives), Commission (for regulations), national competent authorities

Steps:
1. **Transposition** (directives only): member states adopt national implementation measures within the deadline.
   - Commission monitors transposition.
   - Non-transposition → `/infringement` procedure.
2. **Delegated and implementing acts**: Commission adopts subordinate legislation filling in technical details.
   - Delegated acts: EP + Council have right of scrutiny.
   - Implementing acts: comitology committee procedure.
3. **Guidance**: Commission publishes implementation guidance, FAQ, notices.
4. **Enforcement**: member states enforce; Commission monitors and may intervene.

**Output:** Transposition status matrix; delegated/implementing acts adopted; enforcement actions opened.

---

## Phase 7 — Monitoring and evaluation

**Timing:** Typically 3-5 years after entry into force.

Steps:
1. **Monitoring:** Track key indicators defined in the legislative text.
2. **Ex-post evaluation** (REFIT): assess whether the measure achieved its objectives; identify simplification opportunities.
3. Stakeholder consultation on evaluation results.
4. Staff Working Document — evaluation report (SWD).
5. Commission decision: maintain / amend / repeal.
6. If amendment needed → new policy cycle begins.

**Output:** Evaluation SWD + REFIT scoreboard update + Commission decision on follow-up.

---

## Feedback loops

```
Evaluation ──────────────────────────────────► Agenda-setting (new cycle)
     ▲                                                      │
     │                                                      ▼
  Monitoring ◄── Implementation ◄── Adoption ◄── Preparation
```

The EU policy cycle is iterative — each evaluation feeds into the next initiative, either as an amendment, repeal, or successor regulation.
