# OLAF — European Anti-Fraud Office
## Office européen de lutte antifraude

> **Note on institutional status:** OLAF is not a standard DG. It is an
> independent investigative office within the Commission, established by
> Commission Decision 1999/352/EC and governed by Regulation (EU, Euratom)
> 883/2013 as amended by Regulation (EU, Euratom) 2020/2223. OLAF's Director-
> General exercises the investigative function with full independence — neither
> the Commission nor the Commissioner for Budget and Anti-Fraud may instruct
> OLAF on the opening, conduct, or outcome of individual investigations.

---

## Mandate

OLAF's mandate has three distinct limbs:

**1. Administrative investigations** — Conduct internal investigations within
EU institutions, bodies, offices, and agencies (IBOA) and external investigations
into economic operators (companies, individuals) who have misused EU funds or
engaged in fraud affecting the EU budget.

**2. Assistance to member states** — Coordinate and support member state
anti-fraud activities in areas covered by EU law; provide analytical intelligence
and operational support to national authorities investigating fraud against EU
financial interests.

**3. Anti-fraud policy** — Contribute to the design and evaluation of the
Commission's anti-fraud strategy; issue recommendations to EU institutions
and member states to strengthen the protection of EU financial interests.

**Legal basis:** Article 325 TFEU (protection of EU financial interests),
Regulation (EU, Euratom) 883/2013 (OLAF Regulation), Protocol on the
Privileges and Immunities of the EU (investigative access).

## What OLAF can and cannot do

### Can do
- Open an investigation on its own initiative or on the basis of a complaint
  or information from any source (Art. 5 Reg. 883/2013)
- Access all documents, data, and premises of EU institutions and member states'
  authorities holding EU funds (Art. 7)
- Interview EU staff and, with national authority cooperation, external persons (Art. 9)
- Conduct on-the-spot checks at economic operators' premises in member states,
  with the cooperation of national authorities (Art. 3)
- Issue final investigation reports with findings and recommendations (Art. 11)
- Refer cases to EPPO (European Public Prosecutor's Office) where criminal
  conduct is suspected in EU member states participating in EPPO (Art. 12c)
- Recommend disciplinary action, financial recovery, or judicial proceedings to
  the relevant institution, body, or national authority

### Cannot do
- Issue criminal charges or impose criminal penalties — OLAF conducts
  **administrative** investigations; criminal prosecution belongs to EPPO
  or national authorities
- Compel testimony from persons who are not EU staff (cooperation with national
  authorities is required for external persons)
- Freeze assets or impose precautionary measures — these require judicial authority
- Access material covered by professional legal privilege (AM & S principle applies)
- Continue an investigation that EPPO has taken over under Art. 12e Reg. 883/2013
  (primacy of EPPO once it opens a case on the same facts)

## Key procedures

### Investigation lifecycle

```
INFORMATION/COMPLAINT RECEIVED
        │
        ▼
ADMISSIBILITY CHECK (Art. 5)
  Does the information concern EU financial interests?
  Is there sufficient suspicion?
  ├─ No → close / forward to competent authority
  └─ Yes ↓

INVESTIGATION OPENED
  Director-General decision (internal) or
  Supervisory Committee notified (both internal and external investigations)
        │
        ▼
INVESTIGATION PHASE
  Document review, on-the-spot checks, interviews
  Coordination with EPPO / national authorities as needed
        │
        ▼
ARTICLE 9(4) NOTIFICATION (internal investigations)
  The person concerned is informed before the investigation is closed
  and has the opportunity to comment on the facts
        │
        ▼
FINAL REPORT (Art. 11)
  Findings of fact + legal assessment + recommendations
  Sent to: relevant institution/IBOA + national judicial authorities
           (if criminal referral) + EPPO (if EPPO jurisdiction)
        │
        ▼
FOLLOW-UP MONITORING
  OLAF monitors whether institutions/member states have acted on recommendations
  Annual report to EP, Council, Court of Auditors, and Court of Justice
```

### Relationship with EPPO (since June 2021)

OLAF and the European Public Prosecutor's Office have complementary but distinct
jurisdictions. OLAF must notify EPPO without delay when it has reasonable grounds
to suspect a criminal offence within EPPO's competence (Art. 24 Regulation
2017/1939). Once EPPO opens a case, OLAF must refrain from conducting a parallel
investigation on the same facts unless EPPO requests OLAF's support.

Member states not participating in EPPO (currently Denmark, Hungary, Poland,
Sweden): OLAF retains full jurisdiction including criminal referrals to national
authorities.

## Key outputs

- **Final investigation report** (Art. 11 Reg. 883/2013) — findings of fact,
  legal assessment, recommendations for judicial/disciplinary/financial action
- **Preliminary analysis note** — internal assessment of whether an investigation
  should be opened
- **Referral to EPPO** — transmitted when criminal conduct is suspected and EPPO
  has jurisdiction
- **Judicial referral** — to national judicial authorities (non-EPPO member states)
- **Disciplinary recommendation** — to the relevant EU institution or IBOA
- **Recovery recommendation** — to the Commission or paying agency to initiate
  financial recovery under the applicable sectoral rules
- **Intelligence report** — fraud pattern analysis for the anti-fraud policy function
- **OLAF Annual Report** — public report to EP, Council, Court of Auditors

| EPPO | Notification and case-handover obligation — OLAF supports EPPO investigations when requested |

## Supervisory Committee

The Supervisory Committee is an independent body that monitors OLAF's
investigative function (not its policy function) to ensure that the exercise
of investigative powers complies with Regulation 883/2013. It does not direct
investigations but can access all OLAF files and issue opinions.

Composition: five external members, appointed by EP/Council/Commission jointly,
serving 5-year non-renewable terms.

## Commissioner oversight

The Commissioner for **Budget and Anti-Fraud** has political responsibility for
OLAF's institutional position and anti-fraud policy but cannot instruct OLAF on
individual investigation decisions. This operational independence is a structural
guarantee under Art. 6 Regulation 883/2013 and is essential to OLAF's legal
mandate and credibility before the courts.

Supporting DG for budget policy and financial regulation: **DG BUDG**.
