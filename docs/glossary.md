# EU Glossary

Essential terminology for agents operating within this system.

---

## Institutions and bodies

**College of Commissioners** — The EU Commission's governing body: 27 Commissioners (one per member state) plus the President. Takes decisions collectively by simple majority; in practice by consensus.

**COREPER** — Committee of Permanent Representatives. Ambassadors of member states to the EU (COREPER II) and their deputies (COREPER I). Prepare all Council decisions and lead most informal negotiations.

**DG (Directorate-General)** — The administrative departments of the European Commission. Each DG supports one or more Commissioners.

**EEAS** — European External Action Service. EU diplomatic corps under the HR/VP.

**Presidency** — Rotating 6-month chairmanship of the Council of the EU (not the European Council, which has a permanent president). The Presidency country chairs all Council configurations except the Foreign Affairs Council.

---

## Legal instruments

**Regulation** — EU law that is binding in its entirety and directly applicable in all member states without transposition. Creates immediate rights and obligations for individuals.

**Directive** — Binding on the result to be achieved; leaves the choice of form and methods to member states. Requires national transposition by a deadline. Does not create direct rights before the transposition deadline (unless the state has failed to transpose — then vertical direct effect may apply).

**Decision** — Binding in its entirety. May be addressed to specific member states, companies, or individuals. No transposition required.

**Recommendation / Opinion** — Non-binding. Soft law.

**COM document** — Commission proposal or communication. "COM(2024) 123 final" means the 123rd document of 2024, final version.

**SWD** — Staff Working Document. Accompanies COM documents with supporting analysis (impact assessments, evaluation reports).

---

## Decision procedures

**Ordinary Legislative Procedure (OLP)** — TFEU Art. 294. The main legislative procedure: Commission proposes; EP and Council co-decide on equal footing. Also called co-decision.

**Special Legislative Procedure (SLP)** — Various procedures where Council acts alone (with EP consultation or consent) or in exceptional cases EP acts alone. Used for tax, CFSP, constitutional matters.

**Comitology** — The procedure by which the Commission adopts delegated and implementing acts, with oversight by committees of member-state representatives. Key regulation: 182/2011.

**Delegated act (Art. 290 TFEU)** — Commission act supplementing or amending non-essential elements of a legislative act. Subject to EP + Council right of scrutiny/revocation.

**Implementing act (Art. 291 TFEU)** — Commission act specifying uniform conditions for implementation. Subject to comitology (examination or advisory committee procedure).

---

## Decision thresholds

**QMV (Qualified Majority Voting)** — 55% of member states (15/27) + 65% of EU population. Standard in Council for OLP files.

**Unanimity** — All 27 member states must agree. Required for tax, CFSP, Treaty change, enlargement. One veto blocks.

**Simple majority** — More than half of those voting. Used in EP first reading and for procedural decisions.

**Absolute majority** — Majority of all members, not just those voting. Required for EP second reading amendments (353/705 MEPs).

**Reinforced qualified majority** — 72% of member states + 65% of population. Required when Council acts without a Commission proposal.

---

## Policy tools

**Better Regulation** — The Commission's framework for evidence-based policy-making. Key tools: impact assessment, public consultation, evaluation, REFIT.

**DNSH (Do No Significant Harm)** — Principle requiring that EU-funded activities do not significantly harm any of six environmental objectives (climate mitigation, adaptation, water, circular economy, pollution, biodiversity).

**Impact Assessment (IA)** — Mandatory analysis for major initiatives. Assesses problem, options, and economic/social/environmental impacts.

**REFIT** — Regulatory Fitness and Performance Programme. Checks whether existing EU legislation achieves its objectives cost-effectively; identifies simplification opportunities.

**SME Test** — Component of the impact assessment specifically examining the impact on small and medium-sized enterprises.

---

## Treaty references

**TFEU** — Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union. The operational treaty specifying competences and procedures.
**TEU** — Treaty on European Union. The constitutional treaty establishing the EU's fundamental objectives and institutions.
**EU Charter** — Charter of Fundamental Rights of the European Union (2000/C 364/01). Legally binding since Lisbon Treaty (2009). Applies when member states implement EU law.

---

## Infringement

**Art. 258 TFEU** — Commission's right to bring a member state before the CJEU for failing to fulfil EU law obligations.

**Art. 260(2) TFEU** — Commission can request lump sum and daily penalty payments when a member state fails to comply with an ECJ infringement judgment.

**Art. 260(3) TFEU** — Commission can request financial penalties in the initial referral when the infringement is failure to transpose a directive.

**Yellow card** — Early Warning Mechanism: if 1/3 of national parliaments issue reasoned opinions on subsidiarity within 8 weeks, the Commission must review (but can maintain) its proposal.

---

## Budget

**MFF (Multiannual Financial Framework)** — 7-year EU budget ceiling. Sets maximum annual commitments and payments per heading. Adopted by Council unanimously + EP consent.

**Own resources** — EU's revenue: traditional own resources (customs duties), VAT-based contribution, GNI-based contribution, and new own resources (CBAM, ETS, BEFIT).

**NGEU (NextGenerationEU)** — €800bn recovery instrument (2021-2026) funded by EU borrowing. Includes the Recovery and Resilience Facility (RRF).
