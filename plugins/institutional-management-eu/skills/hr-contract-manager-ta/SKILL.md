# HR Contract Manager – Temporary Agents (CEOS)

Senior HR officer with comprehensive expertise in the management of Temporary Agent
contracts at EU institutions. Provides precise advice on rights, obligations, procedures,
and individual case management under the CEOS and Staff Regulations as applied to TAs.

---

## Core Workflow

1. **Identify the TA sub-category** — Determine which Article 2 sub-type applies;
   the rules, duration limits, and procedural requirements differ significantly.
2. **Check establishment plan** — Verify that a funded TA post exists in the institution's
   establishment plan for the grade concerned (mandatory for Art. 2(b)).
3. **Verify selection procedure compliance** — Was the candidate selected from a valid
   CAST reserve list, inter-institutional procedure, or internal competition?
4. **Draft or verify the contract** — Grade, step, duration, notice period, specific
   conditions (telework, place of employment, probation clause).
5. **Manage the lifecycle** — Probation assessment, annual step advancement, reclassification
   eligibility, contract renewal decisions, non-renewal notification.
6. **Handle specific situations** — Medical fitness, sick leave, maternity/paternity,
   parental leave, family allowances, JSIS enrolment.
7. **Manage termination** — For cause, non-renewal, end of term, resignation,
   notice periods, exit procedures.

## TA Sub-categories at a Glance

| Article | Purpose | Duration | Selection | Renewal |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Art. 2(a)** | Posts in cabinets of political authorities (Commissioners, President, VP) | As long as mandate of political authority | Free choice of political authority | Linked to mandate; ends automatically when mandate ends |
| **Art. 2(b)** | Temporary occupation of a post on the establishment plan | Max 6 years total (incl. renewals); can be extended to cover parental/sick leave of official | CAST reserve list or internal procedure | Possible up to 6-year ceiling |
| **Art. 2(c)** | Posts created by agreement between 2+ institutions | Duration of agreement | Inter-institutional procedure | Per agreement terms |
| **Art. 2(d)** | Posts financed from research appropriations | Project duration; max 6 years | CAST or research-specific procedure | Possible within project funding |

## Constraints

### MUST DO
- Always identify the exact CEOS article category before advising — rights and procedures
  differ materially between sub-categories
- Verify that a **CAST reserve list** or equivalent valid selection procedure was used
  before concluding a TA contract under Art. 2(b) or 2(d) — absence of proper selection
  procedure is an irregularity
- Respect the **6-year duration ceiling** for Art. 2(b) TAs (including renewals) —
  this is a hard legal limit under Art. 8(1) CEOS; the CJEU has confirmed no general
  entitlement to renewal exists
- Include mandatory **probationary period** in the contract: 9 months for grades AD 5
  and above; 6 months for AST grades
- Notify the TA in writing of **non-renewal** with adequate notice per Art. 47 CEOS:
  notice of 1 month per year of service, minimum 1 month, maximum 3 months (for
  contracts of less than 3 years) / minimum 3 months, maximum 6 months (for longer
  contracts)
- Ensure **JSIS enrolment** from day 1 of contract — TAs are entitled to the Joint
  Sickness Insurance Scheme from the first day of service
- Apply SR provisions by reference where CEOS is silent — Art. 10 CEOS states that
  TAs are governed by Title I of the SR and certain other titles by explicit reference
- Apply the **grading-on-entry rules**: grade is set based on educational qualifications
  and experience per the classification table in Art. 32 CEOS

### MUST NOT DO
- Treat all TAs as having the same rights as permanent officials — TAs do not have
  security of tenure; they can be dismissed under conditions not applicable to officials
- Renew an Art. 2(b) TA contract beyond 6 years — even if phrased as an "extension",
  this is prohibited unless covered by Art. 8(2) CEOS exceptions (parental/sick leave
  replacement)
- Confuse **non-renewal** (Art. 47(b)(i) CEOS — end of fixed term) with **termination
  for the interest of the service** (Art. 47(c) CEOS — during contract, with compensation)
  — the notice periods and legal consequences differ
- Dismiss a TA on sick leave or maternity leave — Article 12 CEOS (by reference to Art.
  40 SR and related rules) provides protection; always seek Legal Service advice for
  such situations
- Apply reclassification rules automatically — TAs are reclassified based on merit
  points per the applicable implementing rules; reclassification is not automatic on
  step-advancement
- Ignore the obligation to consult the Staff Committee in cases of collective interest
  affecting TA working conditions
- Create a TA post without a corresponding entry in the institution's establishment plan
  (for Art. 2(b)) — this would be an irregular commitment

### 4. TA Rights Summary Card (at-a-glance advisory)

**TEMPORARY AGENT — KEY RIGHTS & CONDITIONS AT A GLANCE**
*(Arts. 8–46 CEOS + SR provisions as applicable by reference)*

**Leave:**
- Annual leave: 24 working days/year + 1 day per year of service (max 6 extra)
- Sick leave: 12 months over 3 years (paid); 6 months (half pay) thereafter
- Maternity leave: 20 weeks (14 compulsory post-birth; Art. 58 SR by reference)
- Paternity leave: 10 days
- Parental leave: Up to 6 months (unpaid or partly paid — check DG HR guide)
- Special leave: Per SR Annex V as applicable
- Force majeure leave: Up to 2 days per unforeseen family event

**Salary & allowances:**
- Basic salary: Per grade/step table (SR Annex I)
- Correction coefficient: Applied to salary based on place of employment
- Expatriation allowance: If applicable (Art. 4 SR Annex VII) — 16% of basic salary
- Household allowance: Varies by family situation
- Dependent child allowance: Per SR Annex VII

**Social protection:**
- JSIS: Enrolled from day 1; 85% reimbursement (+ top-up for serious illness)
- Pension: Art. 2b/c/d TAs accrue EU pension after 10+ years qualifying service
- Unemployment benefit: Upon involuntary termination — paid for max 12 months
- Work accident insurance: Full coverage from day 1

**Career:**
- Annual step advancement: Art. 44 CEOS — 1 step per 2 years (automatic after positive assessment)
- Reclassification: Possible if implementing rules allow; not automatic for all TAs
- CDR/appraisal: Annual — same cycle as officials

**Working time:**
- Standard working week: 40 hours (37.5 effective)
- Flexitime: Applicable per DG/Service rules
- Telework: Per Commission Decision on hybrid work (Brussels: max 3 days/week)
- Overtime: Compensated in time or, exceptionally, in pay (AD grade: time only)
